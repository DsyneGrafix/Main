Pentecost

The Birth of the Church

Celebrating the Coming of the Holy Spirit

What is Pentecost?

"When the day of Pentecost came, they were all together in one place. Suddenly a sound like the blowing of a violent wind came from heaven and filled the whole house where they were sitting. They saw what seemed to be tongues of fire that separated and came to rest on each of them. All of them were filled with the Holy Spirit and began to speak in other tongues as the Spirit enabled them."

Acts 2:1-4

Pentecost marks the moment when Jesus' promise of the Holy Spirit was fulfilled, transforming a small group of disciples into bold witnesses who would turn the world upside down.

🔥

Tongues of Fire

Visible flames rested on each disciple, symbolizing the purifying and empowering presence of the Holy Spirit. This wasn't destructive fire, but the sacred fire of God's presence.

🌪️

Sound of Wind

A violent wind filled the house, representing the powerful, unstoppable movement of God's Spirit. The Hebrew word "ruach" means both wind and spirit.

🗣️

Speaking in Tongues

The disciples spoke in languages they had never learned, enabling people from every nation to hear the gospel in their native tongue. This reversed the confusion of Babel.

The Timeline of Pentecost

Jesus' Promise

Before His ascension, Jesus told the disciples to wait in Jerusalem for the "gift my Father promised" - the Holy Spirit who would give them power to be witnesses.

Before Ascension

10 Days of Prayer

About 120 disciples gathered in the upper room, devoting themselves to prayer and waiting for the promised Spirit. They replaced Judas with Matthias during this time.

10 Days

The Spirit Comes

On the Jewish festival of Pentecost (50 days after Passover), the Holy Spirit came with power, filling all the believers and enabling them to speak in foreign languages.

Pentecost Morning

Peter's Sermon

Peter, filled with boldness, preached the first Christian sermon, explaining that Jesus was the promised Messiah and calling people to repentance.

Same Day

3,000 Baptized

About 3,000 people responded to Peter's message, were baptized, and joined the first Christian church. The kingdom of God exploded into growth.

That Evening

Pentecost Today

The Ongoing Work

Pentecost wasn't a one-time event but the beginning of the Spirit's ongoing work in the church. Today, the same Holy Spirit who empowered the first disciples continues to work through believers, building the church, transforming lives, and spreading the gospel to every nation.

Every believer receives the Holy Spirit at conversion and can experience His power for witnessing, worship, and service.
