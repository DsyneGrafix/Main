Module 1: Historical Foundations Origins ‚Üí Golden Age

Understanding Islam's roots and formative experiences

üèúÔ∏è 1.1 Pre-Islamic Arabia

  • Geography & Trade: Mecca and Yathrib sat on vital caravan routes
  • Tribal Life: Kinship defined identity; honor shaped social order
  • Religious Landscape: Polytheistic with Jewish and Christian communities
Pastoral Lens: Islam's emphasis on monotheism (tawhid) emerges as a response to a fragmented religious environment.

üë§ 1.2 Muhammad's Life (570-632 CE)

  • Early Years: Orphaned, known as "trustworthy," married Khadijah
  • Call to Prophecy: Age 40, revelations in Cave of Hira
  • Hijra (622): Migration to Medina marks Islamic Year 1
  • Return to Mecca: Cleansed the Kaaba of idols
Pastoral Lens: Parallels with biblical prophets help frame conversation, while differences regarding Christ remain central.

Module 2: Core Beliefs & Practices Iman + Islam

The framework shaping Muslim devotion worldwide

üìø 2.1 The Six Articles of Faith (Iman)

  1. God (Allah): Absolute unity‚Äîno partners, no division
  2. Angels: Created from light; Gabriel delivers revelation
  3. Scriptures: Torah, Psalms, Gospel acknowledged; Qur'an final
  4. Prophets: Adam to Muhammad; Jesus honored but not divine
  5. Last Day: Resurrection, judgment, heaven/hell
  6. Divine Decree: God's sovereignty with human responsibility
Pastoral Reflection: Affirm shared belief in God's sovereignty. Clarify differences regarding revelation and Christ's person.

üïå 2.2 The Five Pillars (Islam)

  1. Shahada: "There is no god but Allah; Muhammad is the Messenger"
  2. Salah: Five daily prayers facing Mecca
  3. Zakat: Obligatory almsgiving (2.5% of wealth)
  4. Sawm: Fasting during Ramadan
  5. Hajj: Pilgrimage to Mecca once if able

‚ö†Ô∏è Essential Differences

‚ùì Answering Common Questions

  • "Is Allah the same as the Christian God?" Islam confesses the one Creator; Christianity confesses the same Creator known fully in the Triune God. The difference is real and central, yet conversation can begin with creation and moral order.
  • "Do Muslims worship Muhammad?" No. He is honored as a prophet, not divine. This is a common misconception.
  • "Why don't Muslims accept Jesus as Lord?" Islam's prophetic framework honors Jesus but rejects his deity and crucifixion. Show from Scripture why the cross and resurrection stand at the heart of the gospel.
