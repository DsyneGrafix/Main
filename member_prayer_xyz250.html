The Great Divides: Origins of Division

Two pivotal events in history created the foundational splits in Christianity. These schisms, driven by a mix of theology, politics, and culture, set the stage for the denominational landscape we see today. Explore the timeline to understand these key moments.

1054: The East-West Schism

The formal break between the Western (Roman Catholic) and Eastern (Orthodox) churches. This was the result of centuries of growing political and theological disagreements, particularly over papal authority and the *Filioque* clause in the Nicene Creed. This event solidified two distinct centers of Christian power and tradition.

1517: The Protestant Reformation

Initiated by Martin Luther's 95 Theses, this movement challenged the authority and doctrines of the Roman Catholic Church. Aided by the printing press, the Reformation emphasized "Scripture Alone" (*sola scriptura*), leading to a rejection of papal authority and the creation of thousands of new denominations across Western Europe and beyond.
