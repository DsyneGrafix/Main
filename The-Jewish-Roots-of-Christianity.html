The Foundation of Scripture

The Christian faith did not emerge from a vacuum; its roots are deeply planted in the soil of the Hebrew Bible, which Christians call the Old Testament. This collection of sacred texts forms the vast majority of the biblical canon and provides the context, prophecies, and covenants essential for understanding Jesus and the New Testament.

Jesus, The Jewish Messiah

Jesus of Nazareth was a first-century Jew. He was born into a Jewish family, learned the Hebrew Scriptures, worshiped at the Temple, and taught in synagogues. His life, teachings, and identity can only be fully understood within this rich Jewish context.

A Jewish Rabbi

Jesus was addressed as "Rabbi" (Teacher) by his followers. His teaching methods, such as using parables and asking probing questions, were common among Jewish teachers of his time.

The Davidic King

The title "Messiah" (in Hebrew) or "Christ" (in Greek) means "Anointed One." It refers to the prophesied king from the line of David who would restore Israel. The Gospels emphasize Jesus's Davidic lineage.

Fulfillment of the Law

Jesus declared he came not to abolish the Law (Torah) and the Prophets, but to fulfill them. His teachings often expanded upon the ethical and spiritual intent behind the Mosaic Law.

The Feasts of Israel

God commanded Israel to observe specific feasts throughout the year to remember His mighty acts and promises. Early Christians saw these feasts as "types" or "shadows" pointing forward to the work of the Messiah. Click on each feast to see its significance.

Bridging Key Concepts

Many core tenets of Christianity are expansions of foundational Jewish concepts. The New Testament reinterprets these ideas in light of Jesus, viewing him as their ultimate fulfillment and meaning.

In Judaism

An agreement between God and His people, starting with Abraham and Moses, marked by obedience to the Law (Torah).

Covenant

In Christianity

A "New Covenant" established through Jesus's sacrifice, based on grace through faith, and open to all people, not just ethnic Israel.
