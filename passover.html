On a night over 3,400 years ago, an entire nation found freedom through the blood of a lamb. This pivotal event would forever define God's pattern of redemption.

The Protection

"The blood shall be a sign for you... when I see the blood, I will pass over you."

- Exodus 12:13

The Hebrew word "Pesach" (Passover) literally means "to pass over" or "to spare." It signifies God's protective mercy toward those covered by the blood.

Celebrating Passover as a Believer

Many Christians find deep meaning in celebrating Passover, seeing Christ in every element of this ancient feast.

A Messianic Passover Seder

The Afikomen

The middle matzah is broken, wrapped in linen, hidden, and later "resurrected" - a beautiful picture of Christ's death, burial, and resurrection.

The Third Cup

The Cup of Redemption - this is the cup Jesus blessed at the Last Supper, establishing the New Covenant in His blood.

Elijah's Cup

Set for the prophet who prepares the way for Messiah - fulfilled in John the Baptist and awaiting the Second Coming.
