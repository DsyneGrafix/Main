The 5 Core Principles - Click to Explore

📚

Context is King

Every verse exists within layers of context: immediate paragraph, chapter, book, and the entire biblical canon.

Try it: Philippians 4:13 - "I can do all things..."
In context: Paul speaks of contentment in hardship, not personal achievement!
🎭

Genre Shapes Meaning

Poetry speaks differently than law. Prophecy uses symbols narrative doesn't. Each genre has its own rules.

Example: Psalm 23 is poetry, not a livestock manual. The metaphors paint spiritual truths!
🌍

Original Language Matters

Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek carry nuances that English can't fully capture. Single words can have multiple meanings.

Deep Dive: "Agape" love vs "Phileo" love - understanding Greek unlocks deeper meaning!
✍️

Authorial Intent

What did the author mean to communicate to their original audience? This anchors our interpretation.

Consider: Paul's letters addressed specific church problems - knowing which ones illuminates the text!
🔗

Scripture Interprets Scripture

The Bible is a unified message. Clear passages illuminate difficult ones. Let the whole counsel speak.

Reconcile: James says "faith without works" while Paul says "faith alone" - they complement, not contradict!

Journey Through Hermeneutical History

Early Church (100-500 AD)

Allegorical interpretation dominates. Church fathers like Origen see multiple layers of meaning.

Medieval Period (500-1500)

Four-fold sense of Scripture: literal, allegorical, moral, and anagogical. Scholasticism emerges.

Reformation (1500s)

Luther's "Scripture alone" principle. Return to grammatical-historical method.

Enlightenment (1700s)

Historical-critical method develops. Scientific approach to biblical texts.

Modern Era (1900s-Today)

Multiple approaches coexist: literary, canonical, reader-response, and more.
