The Holy of Holies

God's Throne Room on Earth

Enter the most sacred space in the universe, where heaven and earth meet in divine glory

The Most Sacred Space

The Holy of Holies (קֹדֶשׁ הַקְּדָשִׁים - Qodesh HaQodashim) was the innermost sanctuary of the Tabernacle and later the Temple. Only 15 feet square, this small room contained the very presence of the Almighty God.

No light entered this sacred space except the supernatural glow of God's Shekinah glory. It was here that heaven and earth met, where the infinite God chose to dwell among His finite people.

Sacred Dimensions

  • ◆ 15 x 15 x 15 feet (a perfect cube)
  • ◆ No windows or artificial light
  • ◆ Entered only once per year
  • ◆ Contained the Ark of the Covenant
  • ◆ Covered with pure gold

The Mystery of the Missing Ark

When the Babylonians destroyed Solomon's Temple in 586 BC, the Ark of the Covenant disappeared from history. Various theories suggest it was hidden by Jeremiah, taken to Ethiopia, or destroyed. When the Second Temple was built, the Holy of Holies stood empty except for the Foundation Stone.

Some believe the Ark will reappear in the end times, while others see its absence as pointing to Christ himself as the ultimate mercy seat (Romans 3:25).

Yom Kippur: The Day of Atonement

Once a Year

Only on Yom Kippur could the High Priest enter the Holy of Holies. After elaborate purification rituals, he would pass through the veil carrying the blood of the sacrifice, approaching the mercy seat with trembling reverence.

"And he shall take some of the blood of the bull and sprinkle it with his finger on the front of the mercy seat on the east side, and in front of the mercy seat he shall sprinkle some of the blood with his finger seven times." — Leviticus 16:14

The High Priest's Preparation

  • ✦ Seven days of separation
  • ✦ Special white linen garments
  • ✦ Multiple washings and sacrifices
  • ✦ Bells on his robe (to know he was alive)
  • ✦ A rope around his ankle (in case he died)

The Sacred Ritual

  • ✦ Blood sprinkled on the mercy seat
  • ✦ Incense creating a protective cloud
  • ✦ Atonement for the sanctuary
  • ✦ Atonement for the priesthood
  • ✦ Atonement for all Israel

The Shekinah Glory

God's Visible Presence

"Then the cloud covered the tent of meeting, and the glory of the Lord filled the tabernacle. And Moses was not able to enter the tent of meeting because the cloud settled on it, and the glory of the Lord filled the tabernacle."

— Exodus 40:34-35

Manifestations of Glory

  • ★ Brilliant, unapproachable light
  • ★ Cloud by day, fire by night
  • ★ The divine voice speaking
  • ★ Overwhelming holiness
  • ★ Physical weight of glory

The Departure

Ezekiel witnessed the gradual departure of God's glory from the Temple before its destruction. The glory moved from the Holy of Holies to the threshold, then to the eastern gate, then to the Mount of Olives.

"Then the glory of the Lord went out from the threshold of the house, and stood over the cherubim." — Ezekiel 10:18

The Veil: Barrier Between Worlds

The Sacred Curtain

The veil was an intricately woven curtain, 60 feet high and 30 feet wide, made of blue, purple, and scarlet thread with golden cherubim. This massive barrier separated the Holy Place from the Holy of Holies, representing the separation between God and humanity due to sin.

"And you shall make a veil of blue and purple and scarlet yarns and fine twined linen. It shall be made with cherubim skillfully worked into it." — Exodus 26:31

Symbolic Meaning

  • ◊ Blue: Heavenly nature of God
  • ◊ Purple: Royalty and majesty
  • ◊ Scarlet: Blood sacrifice required
  • ◊ Cherubim: Guardians of holiness
  • ◊ Fine linen: Purity and righteousness

The Great Tearing

"And Jesus cried out again with a loud voice and yielded up his spirit. And behold, the curtain of the temple was torn in two, from top to bottom." — Matthew 27:50-51

At Christ's death, God himself tore the veil from top to bottom, opening the way into His presence for all believers. The barrier was removed forever.

The Eternal Holy of Holies

"And I heard a loud voice from the throne saying, 'Behold, the dwelling place of God is with man. He will dwell with them, and they will be his people, and God himself will be with them as their God.'"

— Revelation 21:3

The ultimate fulfillment of the Holy of Holies is the New Jerusalem, where God will dwell directly with His people forever. No temple will be needed, for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are its temple.
